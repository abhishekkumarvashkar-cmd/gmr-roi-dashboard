Why This Has to Work as a Connected System

Getting a new patient is not a single action. It is a short sequence of steps, and each step depends on the one before it. If any layer is missing, patients can get stuck partway through this sequence - no matter how strong the other layers are.

1
Discovery
Patient searches on Google, a directory, or asks an AI assistant
2
Evaluation
Looks at 2-3 options, checks if each practice looks real and trustworthy
3
Decision
Chooses the most credible option and books, calls, or fills out a form
4
Reinforcement
If experience is good, leaves a review - helping the next patient find and trust you

Each of the four layers in your launchpad corresponds to one of these steps. The system doesn't stop there - what happens after the first appointment, whether experience was positive, whether the patient returns - these reinforce the cycle for the next patient.

Website, SEO & Content

Your Digital Home Base and Growth Engine

Supports: new patient acquisition today, and compounding growth over time

Your website is where every other layer sends patients - so it has to load quickly, look current, explain your specialties clearly, and make it obvious how to book. Your content, blog posts written specifically for your specialty and your target patients, builds search rankings over time and establishes your authority in ways that compound long after they are published.

What's Included
  • A custom, mobile-responsive website with secure patient intake forms, appointment and contact forms, and a patient review display widget
  • On-page SEO collaboration and a page-one ranking strategy for the searches your future patients use
  • Content strategy alignment, so every piece of content supports both SEO and the patients you are trying to reach
  • Monthly blog posts written for your specialty
  • Blog publishing, sharing, and SEO tag optimization
  • Content outreach to build awareness of your expertise beyond your own site
Why This Can't Be Optional This layer is the foundation the other three are built on. A Google Business Profile, a directory listing, or an AI assistant can all point a patient toward your practice, but all of them need a real website to link to. Without it, there is no home base for any of the trust signals the rest of the system builds.
If This Layer Is Weak or Missing Without a strong website, every other layer is sending patients to a dead end. Local search has nothing to rank, directories have nothing useful to link to, reviews have no context, and AI assistants have no authoritative source to pull from.
2

Google Business Profile + Directories

Your Local Discovery Layer

Supports: new patient acquisition, with ongoing value for the patients you already have

This layer makes sure your practice shows up as a real, findable location in each of your target markets, both for patients searching on their own and for referred or returning patients double-checking your details online.

What's Included
  • Google Business Profile setup and optimization: categories, attributes, services, description, and photos
  • Name, address, and phone number kept consistent across 50+ medical directories, including Healthgrades
  • Ongoing keyword and category optimization aimed at page-one, map-pack visibility
  • Up to 4 Google Business Profile posts per month to keep your listing active and current
  • Monitoring and insights reporting on how your listings are performing
Why This Can't Be Optional Across your target markets, this is how a patient finds a local, accurate listing for your practice. It is also how a referral from your training program or professional network gets confirmed - a referred patient will often still look you up before calling. Existing patients rely on the same listing for hours, directions, and contact details, so this layer supports retention as well as acquisition.
If This Layer Is Weak or Missing Even a strong website becomes hard to find, because most local searches are answered first by map results and directory listings, not the website itself. Referred and returning patients looking for your address or hours may find outdated or missing information, and trust begins to erode before they even arrive.

Reputation, Engagement & Community

Building Trust and Keeping Patients Connected

Supports: new patient acquisition through trust, and retention through ongoing engagement

This layer turns a first-time visitor into a booked patient, and a booked patient into someone who returns, refers others, and stays connected to your practice. It has three connected parts.

Online Reputation Management (ORM)
  • Positive review acquisition campaigns, including simple QR-code based tools for patients
  • Review management dashboard with real-time review and feedback notifications
  • Negative review protection and a review balancer
  • Professional response handling for patient feedback
  • Reputation reporting
  • Easy patient uploads
  • EMR integration (optional add-on) - review requests triggered directly from patient records
Patient Engagement Insights

While ORM manages what is public, this part helps you understand what your patients are actually experiencing - one of the clearest ways to catch small issues before they affect retention or show up in reviews.

  • Patient satisfaction timeline, tracking how sentiment changes over time
  • CommentWiz (NLP analysis), surfacing themes in patient feedback automatically
  • Word cloud insights, a quick visual read on what patients are saying most
Social Media & Community Presence

Patients increasingly check social profiles alongside reviews before booking, supporting the same trust-building as ORM while giving existing patients an easy way to stay connected between visits.

  • Profile audit and optimization, or new profile creation if needed, across Facebook, Instagram, X, LinkedIn, and YouTube
  • Up to 10 posts per month to keep your practice visible and human between visits
The Trust Signal Consider two practices with similar websites and credentials. One has 20 reviews averaging 4.8 stars. The other has none. Many patients will choose the first, simply because the reviews feel like evidence that other people have been there, had a positive experience, and found it safe enough to say so publicly. For a specialty where patients are discussing deeply personal concerns, reviews are one of the only signals available to a prospective patient who has no personal referral.
Why This Can't Be Optional For specialties where patients discuss deeply personal concerns, reviews are one of the only signals available to a prospective patient who has no personal referral. Without social media and engagement, there is no ongoing connection with existing patients and no community presence to amplify your visibility between appointments.
If This Layer Is Weak or Missing The other layers can successfully bring patients to your profile, but some will leave without booking because there is nothing yet to show that other patients trust you. The practice may also struggle to retain the patients it does acquire, because there is no system to stay top-of-mind between visits.
4

AI Search Visibility

Where Patients Are Searching Next

Supports: new patient acquisition today, and positioning for where search is headed

AI assistants such as ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot are increasingly part of how patients research providers, and they pull what they know directly from the three layers above: your website, your Google Business Profile and directories, and your reviews. This layer optimizes specifically for AI search visibility and keeps your practice appearing accurately in AI-generated recommendations.

What's Included
  • Technical optimization of your website for AI search visibility
  • AI-optimized content creation
  • Authority building through credible mentions of your practice across the web
  • Local AI search optimization
  • Continuous AI audit and performance monitoring
How Fast AI Search Is Growing According to a national survey by KFF (Kaiser Family Foundation), a significant and growing share of adults report using AI tools to research health information - a trend that has accelerated rapidly in the past two years. For specialties where privacy concerns may prevent patients from asking questions in person or in a traditional search, AI assistants are already playing a meaningful role in where patients go first.
Why This Can't Be Optional This layer depends entirely on the first three being solid. Google's own Search Central documentation states that its AI search features are built on its core search ranking systems, which means the same signals that drive traditional search rankings also shape AI visibility. AI search doesn't replace the first three layers - it amplifies them when they're in place, and fails to surface your practice when they're not.
If This Layer Is Weak or Missing Your practice is unlikely to appear in AI-generated recommendations, or an AI tool may surface outdated or incorrect information about your practice with nothing in place to correct it. As AI search grows as a primary discovery channel for health information, this gap becomes a progressively larger competitive disadvantage.

Your First Year: How It Comes Together

Here is roughly how these layers come online and build on each other over time, from a standing start to a system that supports acquisition, engagement, and growth together.

Months 1-2

Launch

Built From Zero

  • Google Business Profile and directory listings set up and optimized across your target market(s)
  • Social profiles created or updated
  • First round of reviews requested from your existing patients
  • Foundational AI search optimization work begins

Months 2-4

Momentum

  • Monthly blog content begins publishing and getting indexed
  • Review volume starts to grow
  • AI search optimization strengthens how your practice appears in AI assistant results
  • First monthly performance reports show early trends
  • If a paid search accelerant is activated, it begins generating inquiries during this window

Months 4-9

Compounding

  • Organic search rankings begin moving for key local searches across your target markets - competitive specialties typically take longer than a single quarter to gain real traction
  • Reputation signals compound as review count and average rating grow
  • AI search visibility improves as your overall online presence matures
  • Referral flow from your training program or professional network combines with new digital-driven patients
  • Patient engagement insights begin showing meaningful patterns in satisfaction and feedback

Month 9+

Retention & Sustainable Growth

  • Patient satisfaction insights guide real improvements to the patient experience
  • Returning patients and referrals, fueled by your growing reviews and social presence, add to new patients from search and AI
  • Content and social media increasingly reflect real practice milestones and outcomes, within appropriate privacy boundaries, deepening trust
  • Reporting shifts focus from getting found toward retention and long-term growth metrics
  • This is typically when a paid search scaling lever, if not already in use, becomes most valuable - for example when adding a second provider or location

Looking Ahead: Paid Search as a Scaling Lever

Once the four layers are in place and working together, organic visibility, local search, reputation, and AI search compound on their own. Paid search - Google Ads and related paid media - is a separate lever that can accelerate visibility during any window, but works best when the four layers are already in place to convert that paid traffic into booked patients.

Given a goal of building patient volume quickly, this lever can also be activated earlier than usual, in parallel with the four layers above, to generate visibility while the organic system builds momentum. It is entirely optional, and separate from the Google & AI Search Marketing Package.

Where It Fits Paid traffic still depends on the foundation above - your website, Google Business Profile, and reviews - to convert into booked patients. Whether you choose to use it at launch or hold it in reserve as a scaling tool, that foundation has to be in place for it to work.
